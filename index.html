What is Echo?

Echo is comprised of a Web and Mobile Application which implement a unique approach to visualizing auditory information. From our research, we discovered that Virtual Reality is the ultimate medium to immerse someone inside of an audio environment, and will promote the highest level of understanding in our users. Our Web Application will help teach students about complex concepts and provide them with the resources that they need in order to learn about sound wave interaction, specifically for audio engineering and sound design. Our Mobile Application gives students and teachers the ability to visualize sound waves in different audio environments through a unique implementation of Google Cardboard Virtual Reality with Unity.

Echo presents our users the option to select an acoustic environments they would like to visualize sound within, as well as the ability to alter the frequency, amplitude, and other attributes of the sound waves. Echo also allows users to be able to choose how sound should be displayed within the audio environment, whether that be sound wave or particle models. Our goal is to lower the barrier of entry into the audio engineering and sound design industry. Our Web Application complements our Mobile Application by supplying tutorials on how to use the Mobile Application, how to create environments and then visualize them, as well as alternate methods of visualizing sound using 2-dimensional models.

Our Model

Our model is based on a representation of air particles and their interaction with one another within an audio environment. When sound is introduced into the environment the vibration of these particles represents a wave moving through a medium. Frequency is shown by the rate at which the particles move, amplitude is shown by their velocity. In order to show the many frequencies that exist in a piece of music or audio output, the user can filter by a frequency or frequency range to view the entire audible spectrum in our visualization.
